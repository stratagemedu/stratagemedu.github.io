Earning Your Secondary School Diploma

18 Compulsory Credits

In order to obtain a Secondary School Diploma(OSSD) from the Province of Ontario , students must enroll and earn credits in a total of 30 courses made up of 18 Compulsory Courses and 12 Optional Courses. In addition to these courses, each student must successfully write and pass the Provincial Literacy Test.

The requirements for the OSSD are detailed as follows:

  • 4 credits in English (1 credit per grade)
  • 3 credits in mathematics (1 credit in Grade 11 or 12)
  • 2 credits in science
  • 1 credit in Canadian history
  • 1 credit in Canadian geography
  • 1 credit in the arts
  • 1 credit in health and physical education
  • 1 credit in French as a second language
  • 5 credit in career studies
  • 5 credit in civics
  • One Credit from Group 1 : English or French as a second language, a Native language, a classical or an international language, social sciences and the humanities, Canadian and world studies , or Guidance and career education.
  • One Credit from Group 2 : health and physical education, the arts, business studies, or French as a second language.
  • One Credit from Group 3 : Science (Grade 11 or 12), Technological education, French as a second language, or Computer studies.
  • In addition to the above courses, each student pursuing the OSSD must complete: 12 Optional Credits, the Provincial Literacy Test, and 40 Hours of Community Service

Admission Procedure and Academic Assessment

The Ontario Secondary School Curriculum ranks amongst the best worldwide. This combined with the strong track record of the ILC program in successfully developing and delivering its curriculum globally make the demand for this program highly competitive.

At Stratagem Learning, we pride ourselves with the academic success of each of our students. In order to ensure the success of each student enrolling with us, we carefully assess the skills and level of education of each student based on previous academic records. This allows us to guide each student through a program that suits their needs in order to facilitate their success in our program.

For this reason, each prospective student will be required to submit records of education as part of their enrollment process.

In addition to the academic assessment, each student will be required to write and pass English Language Competency Test.
